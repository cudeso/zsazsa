Writing a strong PIR
  • Tie it to a real decision. Capture the decision, the decision-maker and what happens if you don't answer.
  • Anchor it in your organisation. Start from a stakeholder's mission, assets and adversarial operations they care about (ransomware, espionage, fraud, DoS, supply chain).
  • Score it with appeal × impact. Use priority and time-sensitivity together to rank against other PIRs.
  • Make it answerable, not yes/no. Specific, scoped questions yield collection that the analyser and your sources can act on.
  • Bound the scope. Geography, sectors, threat actors, threat types, time-frame and exclusions keep it operational.
  • Plan the deliverable. Format and distribution should match the decision-maker's workflow.
  • Review periodically. Set a next-review date; PIRs should evolve as the threat landscape and the business change.
